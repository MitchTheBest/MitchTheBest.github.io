How to Counter Gank Oriented Heroes

Next Post

One of the most common complaints that you here in low skill matches of Dota 2 is that heroes such as Slark, Riki, Pudge, ect. are broken and need to be nerfed because they stomp games often. This is a mindset issue that all players need to get past in order to get better at the game if they are in these low tier games. All of the "pub stomp" heroes that people complain about all usually have one thing in common, they all excel and benefit from ganking often. Whether it be Pudge, Riki, Bounty Hunter, they all have easy methods for ganking often and without warning for the inexperienced player and make it difficult to do the things necessary to achieve victory. All of these issues stem from a lack of knowledge of how to properly farm and support for whatever position you are playing.

An inexperienced support player with an inexperienced team can easily cost the whole game by not positioning themselves properly and providing the necessary vision and protection for their core heroes. As the support, you are expected and should by out all of the observer wards and promptly place them all game. Just simply warding key points on the map throughout the game, a support player can easily shut down an enemy pudge who does not have a way to get past unseen unless he gets a fortuitous rune or used a smoke which both have limits to how much they can be used and cost something extra to the ganker. Simply warding and dewarding enemy wards will signifigantly reduce the effectiveness of enemy ganks from non-invisible heroes. For invisible heroes it is very important to buy ample sentry wards and dust. In the beginning of game, it is important for the supports to provide defensive sentry wards in every lane to make sure that ganks from invisible heroes are stumped early on and they don't get valuable momentum. Dust should also be bought in the early game so that ganking invisible heroes is easier or possible which will make them think twice about ganking or staying in lane. Into the mid and late game, it is always important to place sentries along with your observer wards to keep track of their movements across the map, as well as keeping extra sentries and dust for when team fights break out. As a support, you should always be warding but when faced with an enemy team composed of gank oriented heroes it is extra important and necessary.

Core players have equally important responsibilities for when facing a gank oriented line up. The core heroes have to know when it is safe to farm, safe to fight, and safe to push. A good rule to follow for if you are a core player is that if no enemy heroes are visible and haven't been for 15-30 seconds you are probably being ganked. Of course, the more vision the team has, the more likely you will be able to notice potential gank attempts. Though, inversely if the enemy team has the vision advantage, you have to compensate and be even more careful than if they had no vision because they will have more information on which fights are safe to take. With pushing it is important to know where the enemy team is but also whether or not the enemies have tp scrolls which can easily be attained by vision. Keeping track of the enemy team, whether or not they are together or what key items they have will also allow you to know when and where to fight.

Many of the considered over powered heroes of low MMR games all have their weakness. It is important for players to adjust their mindset and playstyle instead of just blaming the developers when they could be learning how to counter them. Proper wards and skilled decision making can dramatically change the outcome of many different Dota matches.
